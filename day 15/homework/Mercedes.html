First ever Mercedes

Mercedes-Benz, founded in 1926, has roots that trace back to Karl Benz’s invention of the first gasoline-powered car, the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, in 1886. The brand was officially formed when Benz & Cie. merged with Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft (DMG), whose vehicles were already being marketed under the name “Mercedes.” Over the decades, Mercedes-Benz has become a symbol of luxury, innovation, and engineering excellence, pioneering advancements in safety, performance, and automotive design. The company continues to shape the future of mobility through electric vehicles and autonomous driving technologies.
